Glossary Terms

  • Sire : A sire is a male horse used for breeding. The sire contributes half of the genetic material to the offspring.
  • Dam : A dam is a female horse used for breeding. The dam also contributes half of the genetic material to the offspring.
  • Foal : A foal is a young horse, usually less than one year old, either male or female. It is the result of the mating between a sire and a dam.
  • Colt : A colt is a young male horse that is typically under four years old.
  • Filly : A filly is a young female horse that is typically under four years old.
  • Stallion : A stallion is an uncastrated (intact) adult male horse, typically used for breeding purposes.
  • Mare : A mare is an adult female horse, typically used for breeding purposes.
  • Gelding : A gelding is a castrated male horse. Geldings are often used for riding and driving purposes, but they cannot be used for breeding.
  • Stud : A stud refers to a male horse that is used for breeding purposes. It can also refer to a breeding facility where stallions are kept for breeding.
  • Broodmare : A broodmare is a mare specifically used for breeding purposes. These mares are selected based on their genetic pedigree and ability to produce quality offspring.
  • In-foal : A mare is said to be "in-foal" when she is confirmed to be pregnant and carrying a developing foal.
  • Covering : Covering refers to the act of mating between a stallion and a mare. It is the process of sexual reproduction in horses.
  • Bloodlines : Bloodlines refer to the pedigree or ancestral lineage of a horse. It denotes the lineage of sires and dams that have contributed to the genetic makeup of the horse.
